About me

Hi, I'm Ruotong Mu. I hold a master's degree in International Relations from the Johns Hopkins University School of Advanced International Studies (SAIS), where I specialized in International Economics and Finance and African Studies. Before joining SAIS, I earned a bachelor's degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics (PPE) from Renmin University of China.

My research interests lie at the intersection of international finance and development, with a particular focus on China's overseas investment, sovereign debt sustainability in African countries, and the broader question of how macroeconomic policy can foster sustainable and inclusive development in emerging economies.

These interests were initially shaped by an experience outside the classroom. During the 2021 Henan floods, I volunteered as part of an online emergency rescue network, helping connect affected communities with rescue teams and relief resources. Witnessing both the urgency of humanitarian response and the limits of short-term relief led me to ask a broader question: how can countries build the economic resilience needed to better withstand future crises? That question ultimately sparked my interest in development economics and sustainable macroeconomic policy.

Since then, I have pursued opportunities that bridge policy research and real-world implementation. At the World Resources Institute (WRI) and United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF), I worked on public affairs, stakeholder engagement, and cross-sector partnerships, collaborating with government agencies, international organizations, and research institutions to advance development initiatives. Complementing this policy experience, my work at the South African Reserve Bank (SARB) and the Institute of International Finance (IIF) strengthened my analytical foundation in macroeconomic research, economic forecasting, and data management. There, I contributed to economic publications, policy analysis, and the development of databases used to support forecasting and research.

Today, I am passionate about combining rigorous economic analysis with practical policy solutions to address development challenges. I aspire to contribute to evidence-based policymaking that strengthens macroeconomic resilience, expands sustainable investment, and promotes long-term, inclusive growth across developing economies.
